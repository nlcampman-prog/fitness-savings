## The Key Criteria: What Makes a Colostrum Supplement High-Quality?

When evaluating colostrum, never settle for cheap, mass-market formulations. To get real clinical benefits, a brand must meet these strict criteria:

1.  **Low-Temperature Processing:** The active biomolecules (like immunoglobulins and growth factors) in colostrum are highly heat-sensitive. High-heat spray drying pasteurizes and destroys these proteins, leaving you with nothing more than expensive milk powder. Look for brands that utilize proprietary **cold-process bio-preservation**.
2.  **High Immunoglobulin G (IgG) Content:** IgG is the vital antibody responsible for neutralizing harmful gut pathogens and shielding your immune system. High-quality colostrum should consist of at least 35% to 40% IgG by volume.
3.  **Ethical "Calf-First" Sourcing:** Ethical standards are paramount. Quality brands must operate under strict humane guidelines, ensuring that newborn calves receive their full, necessary share of mother's first-milk before any colostrum is harvested for human consumption.
4.  **100% Grass-Fed & Pasture-Raised:** Sourced from healthy American or New Zealand dairy cows grazing on chemical-free pastures, ensuring a clean, hormone-free profile with no synthetic fillers or gums.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

### Is colostrum safe for individuals with lactose intolerance?
Colostrum contains significantly lower levels of lactose compared to standard transitional dairy milk. Most people with mild lactose sensitivity tolerate **Cowboy Colostrum** exceptionally well. However, because it is a dairy product, those with severe whey or casein allergies should consult their physician before use.

### Can I mix colostrum into hot beverages?
Do not mix colostrum into boiling or extremely hot coffee or tea, as temperatures above 115°F will denature (kill) the highly delicate, bio-active proteins. For best results, mix it into cool water, a morning smoothie, or let your morning coffee cool down to a warm, easily drinkable temperature before stirring in your scoop.
