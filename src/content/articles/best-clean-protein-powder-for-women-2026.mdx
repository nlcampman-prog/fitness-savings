## What Makes a Protein Powder Truly "Clean"?

Before purchasing a protein powder, it is important to read the ingredient label, not just the marketing on the front. A truly clean protein powder should meet the following criteria:

1.  **No Artificial Sweeteners:** Avoid sucralose, aspartame, saccharin, and acesulfame potassium. Instead, look for natural, plant-based sweeteners like organic stevia leaf extract or monk fruit extract—or choose an unflavored/unsweetened option.
2.  **No Gums or Fillers:** Chemical thickeners like xanthan gum, guar gum, and carrageenan are notorious for irritating the gut lining, causing gas and bloating.
3.  **Minimal Ingredients:** The best protein powders have short, recognizable ingredient lists (typically under 5 or 6 items).
4.  **No Soy or Added Sugars:** Look for non-GMO, gluten-free, and soy-free options that won't disrupt delicate hormonal balances.
5.  **High-Quality Protein Sourcing:** Whether animal-based or plant-based, the protein should be sourced ethically and processed at low temperatures to preserve its natural structure and nutritional value.
