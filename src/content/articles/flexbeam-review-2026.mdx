## The Science: How FlexBeam Works (Photobiomodulation)

To understand why the FlexBeam is so effective, you have to understand the science of **Photobiomodulation (PBM)**. When red and near-infrared light hits your skin, it penetrates deep into your cells and interacts with the mitochondria—often called the "powerhouse" of the cell.

Specifically, the light is absorbed by an enzyme called cytochrome c oxidase. This interaction triggers several beneficial biological processes:

1.  **Increased ATP Production:** ATP is the primary energy currency of your cells. By boosting its production, FlexBeam gives your cells the "fuel" they need to repair damage and reduce inflammation faster.
2.  **Hormetic Response:** The light creates a mild, beneficial stressor that activates your body's natural antioxidant and anti-inflammatory defenses.
3.  **Improved Blood Flow:** RLT stimulates the release of nitric oxide, which dilates blood vessels and improves circulation, bringing fresh oxygen and nutrients to the recovery site.

The FlexBeam is particularly effective because its design allows for **contact application**. Unlike panels, where light scatters as it travels through the air, the FlexBeam's pods sit directly against the skin, ensuring nearly 100% of the emitted energy reaches the target tissue.

## FAQ: Your FlexBeam Questions Answered

### How long should I use the FlexBeam?
Most treatments last 10 minutes per area. The device will automatically beep and turn off when the session is complete. You can treat multiple areas in one session, but avoid over-treating the exact same spot more than twice per day.

### Can I use FlexBeam over my clothes?
For the best results, no. Red and NIR light are most effective when they have direct contact with the skin. Using it over thick clothing will significantly reduce the amount of light energy that reaches your tissues.

### Is the light dangerous to my eyes?
FlexBeam uses non-ionizing radiation, so it's generally safe. However, the NIR light is very bright. You should avoid looking directly into the pods while they are active.

### How often should I use it?
For acute injuries, use it 1-2 times daily. For general recovery and maintenance, 3-5 times per week is usually sufficient.
