When you're pushing through a high-intensity workout, water alone isn't always enough. As you sweat, you lose more than just fluid; you lose essential minerals—electrolytes—that are critical for muscle contraction, nerve function, and maintaining fluid balance.

In 2026, the hydration market has moved beyond sugary sports drinks toward high-potency, low-sugar electrolyte powders that deliver exactly what your body needs to sustain performance and prevent cramping.

## Why Electrolytes Matter for Performance

Electrolytes are minerals that carry an electric charge. The big three for athletes are:

- **Sodium:** Helps maintain fluid balance and prevents muscle cramps.
- **Potassium:** Essential for muscle contractions and heart function.
- **Magnesium:** Supports energy production and muscle relaxation.

Losing these through sweat without replenishing them can lead to fatigue, dizziness, and decreased power output.

## How to Optimize Your Hydration

1. **Pre-Hydrate:** Drink an electrolyte serving 30-45 minutes before a long or intense session.
2. **Intra-Workout:** For sessions longer than 60 minutes, sip on electrolytes to maintain sodium levels.
3. **Listen to Your Body:** If you're getting headaches after training or see salt streaks on your clothes, you likely need a higher-sodium option like **LMNT**.

**Pro Tip:** Avoid drinking *too much* plain water without electrolytes, as it can actually dilute your sodium levels (hyponatremia), leading to decreased performance.
