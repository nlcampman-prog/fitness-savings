## What are Peptides?

Peptides are short chains of amino acids (typically 2 to 50) that act as signaling molecules in the body. They tell your cells to perform specific tasks—such as releasing growth hormones, repairing damaged tissue, or burning fat.

Unlike SARMs or traditional anabolic steroids, which can have significant systemic side effects, peptides are highly targeted. They are "messengers" that work within your body's existing biological frameworks.
