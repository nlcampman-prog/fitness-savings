## What Makes a Good Budget Running Shoe?

Before we get to the list, it's important to know what you’re looking for. In 2026, even a "cheap" shoe should meet these three criteria:

1.  **Cushioning:** You want a responsive foam that absorbs impact without feeling like a brick. Look for EVA or TPU-based midsoles.
2.  **Durability:** The outsole should have enough rubber coverage to withstand at least 300–400 miles of road use.
3.  **Weight:** Modern budget shoes should be light. If it feels heavy in your hand, it’s going to feel like a lead weight on mile four.

## FAQ: Budget Running Shoe Advice

### Can I run a marathon in budget shoes?
Absolutely. While premium carbon-plated "super shoes" can shave off a few seconds, a well-built budget shoe like the Run Star Speed has more than enough cushioning to get you through 26.2 miles safely.

### How often should I replace budget shoes?
Most budget shoes will last between 300 and 400 miles. If you notice the foam feeling "dead" or you start to feel new aches in your knees, it’s time for a new pair.

### Is Run Star good for wide feet?
Run Star shoes typically have a standard D-width fit. If you have exceptionally wide feet, we recommend sizing up a half-size.
