## What is a Peptide? The Science of Targeted Repair

To understand how peptides accelerate healing, we must look at how cells communicate:

*   **Short-Chain Amino Acids:** Peptides are short chains of amino acids (typically containing between 2 and 50 amino acids linked together). Think of them as smaller, highly specialized versions of proteins.
*   **Cellular Signaling Messengers:** Unlike dietary protein, which is digested and broken down for raw energy, peptides act as precise **cellular keys**. They bind to specific receptors on cell surfaces, sending instructions to regulate gene expression, boost growth hormone, stimulate blood vessel growth, or trigger collagen synthesis.
*   **Targeted Tissue Repair:** Because peptides are highly specific, they can target and repair specialized tissues—such as accelerating tendon-to-bone healing—without causing the systemic hormonal side effects associated with synthetic anabolic compounds.
