## The Problem with Fixed Bars

Traditional gym equipment is built with fixed, straight bars. While effective, they force your wrists, elbows, and shoulders into rigid positions. Over years of heavy lifting, this lack of natural rotation can lead to:

*   **Tendonitis:** Specifically in the elbows (Golfer’s or Tennis elbow).
*   **Shoulder Impingement:** Due to forced internal rotation during pull-ups and rows.
*   **Grip Bottlenecks:** Your hands are forced into a specific angle that might not be your strongest.

Angles 90 solves this by introducing a **dynamic grip**. Instead of your body conforming to the bar, the handle conforms to your body.

## Features of Angles 90 Dynamic Grips

At first glance, they look simple. But the engineering behind Angles 90 is what makes them a "game changer" for grip training for lifting.

### 1. Ergonomic Handle Design
The handles are curved to fit the natural contours of the human hand. This increases the surface area of your grip, which actually allows you to squeeze harder and engage more muscle fibers in your forearms and lats.

### 2. Natural Rotation
This is the "secret sauce." Because the grips are attached via a heavy-duty strap, they can rotate 360 degrees. This allows you to start a row with a neutral grip and finish with a supinated (palms up) grip, mimicking the natural path of your joints.

### 3. Multiple Grip Methods
You can use Angles 90 in three distinct ways:
*   **Ergo Grip:** For maximum comfort and joint protection.
*   **Power Grip:** For heavy pulling where you need the most security.
*   **Lat Grip:** Widens the handles to hit different parts of your back.
