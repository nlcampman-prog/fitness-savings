## What to Look For in High-Performance Leggings

Before buying your next pair of leggings, ensure they meet these three essential criteria:

1.  **Squat-Proof Thickness:** Ensure the fabric is dense enough to provide 100% opaque coverage, even when fully stretched during a deep squat or down-dog pose.
2.  **No-Slip High-Waistband:** Look for a supportive, wide high-rise waistband that stays securely in place without rolling down or pinching during dynamic movements.
3.  **Moisture-Wicking Fabric:** High-quality synthetic blends (nylon/spandex or polyester/spandex) are essential to draw sweat away from your skin, keeping you cool, dry, and chafe-free.
4.  **Flatlock Seams:** Flat, reinforced seams are critical to eliminate skin friction and chafing during high-intensity workouts.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why do my workout leggings keep rolling down?
Leggings typically roll down at the waist for two reasons: they are either too small, causing the fabric to overstretch and pull downward, or the waistband lacks a supportive structure (like an internal elastic band or high-rise taper). To prevent rolling, look for a high-rise cut with a double-layered waistband that tapers slightly toward your natural waist.

### How do I care for my activewear to prevent pilling?
To keep your workout leggings looking and feeling brand-new for years, follow these laundry guidelines:
1.  **Wash on Cold:** Always wash your activewear in cold water on a gentle cycle.
2.  **Avoid Fabric Softener:** Fabric softener coats the synthetic fibers, clogging the weave and destroying the fabric’s moisture-wicking and stretch capabilities.
3.  **Air Dry Only:** High heat from a clothes dryer damages the elastane/spandex fibers, causing your leggings to lose their shape and elasticity. Always hang or lay flat to dry.
